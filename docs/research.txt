Important GitHub nuance
A GitHub organization is not actually nested under a personal account or another organization in the ownership sense. GitHub’s account types are separate: personal accounts, organization accounts, and enterprise accounts. Organizations are shared accounts; people sign in with personal accounts and can own or administer organizations. If you want true central management of multiple organizations, that is the role of a GitHub enterprise account, not one org “inside” another. 

So the practical interpretation of your idea is:

softwarewrighter is your main identity / top-level brand

hardwarewrighter is a separate org you control

both can coexist without introducing another unrelated top-level brand

you manage them from your personal/admin access, rather than by literally nesting one under the other.

This gives you:

one clear company/owner identity: softwarewrighter

one clear software umbrella: sw-embed

one clear hardware umbrella: hardwarewrighter

That matches your actual architecture:

COR24 becomes just one family among several, rather than the top-level container
